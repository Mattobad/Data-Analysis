    Finding cause of employee's resignation: Analyzing role of dissatisfaction in the employee exit.¶

    Introduction¶

    In this notebook, we're going to analyze the exit surveys from employees of Department of Education, Training and Employment(DETE) and the Technical and Further Eduction (TAFE) institute in Queensland, Australia. We'll be analyzing these datasets combinedly to get the answer for following questions:

    • Are employees who only worked for the institutes for a short period of time resigning due to some kind of dissatisfaction? What about employees who have been there longer?

    • Are younger employees resigning due to some kind of dissatisfaction? What about older employees?

    So, in order answer our question we'll be going to perform different data cleaning and data wrangling techniques to get most out of the data. We'll be combining the data from both the source to get the overall idea of exit behavior of the employees. How dissatisfaction plays a role in the employee exit.

    Summary of the results

    After analyzing the exit surveys from employees of Department of Education, Training and Employment(DETE) and the Technical and Further Eduction (TAFE) institute in Queensland, Australia,we got an overview of how much dissatisfaction can play a role in the self-termination of the job by the employees.

    We came across the fact that employees with longer length of employment were more likely to leave the job compared to newer employees due to work related dissatisfaction. Veteran employees are twice likely to leave a job due to dissatisfaction compared to new employees.Similar was true for different age group, older employees are more likely to leave the job due to work related dissatisfaction. There is only 12%(approx.) chances more that senior adult(56 and above) to leave the job compared to youth(below 25 age).

    For details, please follow the full analysis.

    def service_category(val):
    """

    def age_category(age):
    """
    Function  to return the age tag based upon age value
    
    Args:
        age: age 
    
    Returns:
        experience tag based on year of work
    """
    if pd.isnull(age):
        return np.nan
    elif age < 25:
        return 'youth'
    elif age>25 and age <35:
        return 'senior youth'
    elif age>35 and age<45:
        return 'junior adult'
    elif age>45 and age<55:
        return 'adult'      
    else:
        return 'senior adult'

    In this notebook, we analyzed the exit surveys from employees of Department of Education, Training and Employment(DETE) and the Technical and Further Eduction (TAFE) institute in Queensland, Australia. We used this datasets to analysis about how much dissatisfaction can play a role in the self-termination of the job by the employees.

    We came across the fact that employees with longer length of employment were more likely to leave the job compared to newer employees due to work related dissatisfaction. Veteran employees are twice likely to leave a job due to dissatisfaction compared to new employees.Similar was true for different age group, older employees are more likely to leave the job due to work related dissatisfaction. There is only 12%(approx.) chances more that senior adult(56 and above) to leave the job compared to youth(below 25 age).
